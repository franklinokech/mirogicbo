Mirogi CBO Services

We at Mirogi CBO believes in better health care for the benefit of the community as a whole. Our services are tailored to ensure communal benefit ad ensure the vulnerable and most disadvantaged members of the community improves their vulnerability status. This prompted us to engage in the following core activities...

Focused Intervention

In partnership with our partners, we offer services offer services that OVC may need in order of the priority to ensure the OVC benefits from the right service important in his/her age cohort.

Collaboration And Networking

We provide linkage and networking to link our OVC to other organisations that provides services that Mirogi CBO currently do not offer to ensure maximum support of our OVCs.

Home Based Care

We offer home visits to PLWHIV to care and councel them on benefits of living positive lives, teaching them on good hygien to combat opportunistic diseases.

Community Surveillance

We provides watchdog services to OVC who may be victim of marginalisation and exploitation. Mirogi CBO handles defilement cases and reports such cases to the relevent authorities for action.

Engaging In Income Generating Activities

Mirogi CBO operates in a community with high dependency ratio in variou households.Caregivers finds it difficult to pay for basic ammenities from food, shelter, clothing to education fees. This prompted Mirogi CBO to train variou caregivers on modern business and agricultural skills to enable engage in income generating activities. This has led to set up table banking, VSLA and merry go rounds in he community to enhance the saving habits of the caregivers and to help them cater for their basic ammenities

Operation Of Community Phamrmacy

Mirogi CBO through the Bamako Initiative set up a community owned pharmacy and a cosmetic shop to provide medicines to the members of the community as a subsidised price. This has made access to major drugs relatively easy within the community. The phamarcy also boast of managing minor ailments that may affect the community reducing the need to travel to the nearby health facilities
